# Language Endangerment Status Analysis

## 1. Dataset Overview
This dataset contains information on 8,612 languages worldwide, exploring their geographic locations, macroareas, linguistic families, and endangerment status. The primary goal is to understand global language vitality and distribution patterns.

## 2. Data Quality Notes
*   **Missing Values:** `iso639p3code` has the highest missing rate (~8.8%), followed by `status_code` and `status_label` (~5.5%). `latitude` and `longitude` are missing in ~3.6% of records.
*   **Completeness:** The target variables (`status_code`, `status_label`) are mostly complete, allowing for robust analysis of the ~8,100 labeled languages.
*   **Data Types:** Appropriate variable types are used, including boolean flags for isolates and numerical coordinates for mapping.

## 5. Key Insights
*   **Language Endangerment Status Distribution:** "Not endangered" is the most common single classification, accounting for roughly a third (2,704) of the 8,138 languages with a documented status. However, a wide spread of vulnerability exists across the dataset. Nearly 5.5% of the languages in the dataset are missing their endangerment status labels, which could slightly skew the true global distribution.
*   **Geographical Distribution:** Language densities are heavily concentrated in specific global regions, most prominently reflecting areas like Africa and Papunesia. Endangerment statuses visually cluster together, suggesting that geographic proximity strongly influences a language's likelihood of survival.
*   **Endangerment by Region and Family:** The proportion of safe to endangered languages varies drastically by macroarea, pointing to localized extinction crises. While major language families like Atlantic-Congo dominate the dataset, their internal distributions of endangerment vary wildly.
*   **Isolates:** The overwhelming majority of documented languages belong to a language family, while isolated languages form a very small minority. Lacking broader linguistic support networks, their endangerment profiles likely reflect higher vulnerability.
*   **Modeling Performance:** The predictive baseline model struggles significantly to classify intermediate endangerment stages like "moribund" (0.08 F1-score) and "nearly extinct" (0.15 F1-score), suggesting these categories possess highly overlapping geographic and linguistic features.

## 6. Recommendations / Next Steps
*   Impute or further investigate the missing target labels (`status_code`/`status_label`) to ensure a completely accurate picture of global linguistic vitality.
*   Develop specialized, localized conservation strategies specifically targeting language isolates, given their unique vulnerability and lack of familial linguistic ties.
*   Explore advanced modeling techniques or additional socioeconomic features to improve the classification performance on intermediate endangerment categories (e.g., "moribund", "nearly extinct").
*   Conduct deep-dive regional analyses into macroareas showing disproportionately high rates of language extinction to uncover underlying sociopolitical causes.
